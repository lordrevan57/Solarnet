New Athens

Also known as Heliopolis

Cultural capital of the Consortium, seat of the New School, and the city the Solar War chose to make an example of. The Neoparthenon stood at its center for over a century before Jaymeson and Cera Nicks burned it to the ground.

Consortium Earth Location First Trilogy Era Second Renaissance Terran Humanism

Overview

New Athens, also known by its popular alternative name Heliopolis, is one of the most significant cities on Earth during the First Trilogy Era. Located in Greece on the Mediterranean coast in the shadow of Mount Olympus, it serves as the Consortium's cultural, educational, and industrial capital — the city the Consortium built to demonstrate to the world what the new civilization intended to be. It is home to one of Earth's largest spaceports, the founding campus of the IPS Academy, the New School, and the Ecumenical Council for Terran Humanism.

New Athens is, above all, a city of symbolic weight. It was rebuilt to look like the future imagined through the lens of the ancient world — Greco-Roman revivalism expressed through Deco-Futurist architecture, colonnades and spires standing together in a city that was quite deliberately designed to mean something. The Consortium chose Athens for this purpose consciously. The classical inheritance — democracy, philosophy, the ideal of the citizen — was the story the Consortium wanted to tell about itself. New Athens was that story made stone.

History

Foundation Period — Pre-Zero Day

During the economic turmoil of the Foundation Period, Greece was struck particularly hard. Unable to maintain a standing government for longer than a few months at a time, Greece experienced an ancient pattern repeating itself: cities broke from the state and began operating as independent entities. Athens saw sustained street fighting as competing military factions contested the city. The Aegean port infrastructure, the Olympus region, and the administrative districts all changed hands multiple times in the decades before Zero Day. The city that would be rebuilt as the Consortium's showpiece spent its last years as a ruin.

With the signing of the Zero Day Accords and the formal founding of the Consortium, the economic mobilization that followed was faster than anything in recorded history. Athens was selected almost immediately as the site for the Consortium's flagship reconstruction project. The rebuilding was total. The city was not restored — it was replaced, reimagined in a Greco-Roman revivalist style that drew heavily on Sebastian Myrcenae McRae's emerging Deco-Futurism: colonnaded facades on glass-and-steel structures, grand axial boulevards, public spaces designed at a scale intended to communicate institutional confidence. In SY 24, the city held a commencement ceremony in honor of Zero Day itself. The city was said to represent the Consortium and the new beginning for humanity that it heralded. It was officially renamed New Athens on that day.

Second Renaissance — SY 0 to SY 90s

New Athens became a center of commerce, education, and cultural production almost immediately after its completion. The Consortium established its second Integrated Production Sphere Academy in the city, seeding the local population with the Consortium's ideal of educated civic participation. The IPS Academy drew scholars from across Earth and, eventually, from the Belt settlements, making New Athens a meeting point for the inner and outer solar system's intellectual classes before the Second Renaissance had fully taken hold elsewhere.

In the mid SY 90s, the New School was founded as a direct outgrowth of the Academy — an institution that would become, unofficially but effectively, the ideological organ of the Consortium's academic establishment. The New School redubbed the city Heliopolis in honor of the Sun, reflecting the institution's deepening interest in solar symbolism and classical cosmology. The name never achieved official status but entered common usage alongside New Athens, and both names have coexisted in the public imagination ever since. Citizens and spacers who know the city well use both interchangeably; which name a person uses tends to say something about which aspect of the city they feel most connected to.

In the wake of the New School, the Ecumenical Council for Terran Humanism broke away as an independent organization, establishing its own institutional base in the city. The construction of the Heliopolis — the great shrine complex near Mount Olympus that would eventually house altars to the entire constructed Terran Humanist pantheon — began during this period. The Neoparthenon, the city's central ceremonial building, was completed sometime in the SY 100s and became the spiritual and symbolic heart of New Athens. A vast statue of Anthropos Rex was erected on Mount Olympus, visible from the city on clear days; its duplicate stood inside the Neoparthenon alongside statues of the other Terran Humanist deities.

Solar War — SY 129–138

New Athens enters the Solar War as the symbolic capital of everything the Protectorate and the Belt Army Faction have been fighting against: the comfortable institutional heart of a Consortium that built an empire from the labor of the Belt and the compliance of Earth's population. When Jaymeson and Cera Nicks are selected for the infiltration mission to Earth in SY 135, the targeting of the Neoparthenon is deliberate and precise. It is not the city they mean to wound — it is the symbol.

The operation proceeds as follows: the united Protectorate strike force approaches Earth from high above the ecliptic to avoid detection, coordinating with Luna's declaration of defection timed to the fleet's arrival. A wave of kinetic weapons descends on Earth's low orbit support network — satellite platforms, weapons installations — and the resulting debris field provides cover for the surface insertion. Jaymeson and Cera enter the atmosphere separately, land near the Greek coast in the Aegean Sea, and are met by their contact Søren Decks, who secures their transport through the city and arranges extraction at Kalamata spaceport.

They burn the Neoparthenon. They light Mount Olympus ablaze. Then they flee south to Kalamata disguised as CONPOL, achieve orbit, and rejoin the Protectorate fleet. The BAF's parallel operation, Operation Blackheart, runs simultaneously — targeting World Congress members and attempting an assassination of Marcus Cato Scaevola. Søren Decks remains on Earth, choosing to stay to be of further use to the Protectorate cause.

The Neoparthenon's burning is a psychological operation as much as a military one. The intent is to demonstrate to Consortium citizens — to the grounders who have supported this war from comfortable distance — that the conflict has arrived at the center of their world, and that the institutions they believed inviolable are not. Whether it achieves its purpose is a matter the archive records with some ambiguity. What is certain is that the Neoparthenon does not survive the Solar War.

Points of Interest

The Neoparthenon

The central ceremonial building of New Athens and the symbolic heart of Terran Humanist worship on Earth. Constructed in the SY 100s as a successor to the ancient Parthenon on the Acropolis, it was built to an entirely different scale — a vast colonnaded structure at the city's center, housing the full pantheon of Terran Humanist deities. The central statue is a duplicate of the Anthropos Rex figure on Mount Olympus. Burned by Jaymeson and Cera Nicks during the Battle of Earth, SY 135. Not rebuilt during the First Trilogy Era.

The Heliopolis

The great shrine complex constructed by the New School and the Ecumenical Council for Terran Humanism, situated near Mount Olympus. Cathedral-style in its organization: a central altar of Helios at the apex, with altars for each deity of the Terran Humanist pantheon arranged circumferentially in a mirror of the solar system's structure. Parishioners are free to worship any and all altars at leisure. The most widely visited center of Terran Humanist worship anywhere on Earth. Gives rise to the city's alternative name.

Mount Olympus

The original Olympus stands above and behind the city, visible on clear days. The Anthropos Rex statue erected by the New School and the ECTH can be seen from most points in New Athens. The mountain is lit ablaze during the SY 135 operation alongside the burning of the Neoparthenon — a second symbolic act layered atop the first, aimed at the mythological inheritance the Consortium built its identity upon.

The New School

Founded in the mid SY 90s as an outgrowth of the IPS Academy. Unofficially but effectively the ideological organ of Consortium academia. Responsible for the codification of Anglatin as a formal trade and diplomatic language, the construction of the Terran Humanist canon, and the renaming of the city as Heliopolis. Exercises influence over language, art, media, historical interpretation, and timekeeping throughout the Consortium's sphere and beyond. Its influence on Belt and Rim culture is more contested.

Kalamata Spaceport

A major spaceport located south of New Athens along the Peloponnesian coast. Serves as the extraction point for Jaymeson and Cera Nicks following the burning of the Neoparthenon. In the Second Trilogy Era, Kalamata becomes notable as the very spaceport from which Anthrarchos III launches his crew on their outward journey — the same port, centuries later, carrying an entirely different kind of meaning.

✴ Archive Note — HELENA-Prime · Custodian of the Continuity Matrix

New Athens is a city I know primarily through records, not memory. It was rebuilt before I was activated, and my access to it in the SY 3 onward period was through Solarnet feeds, Consortium data transmissions, and the institutional publications of the New School — which were, it should be said, extensive. The New School produced more text per capita than any institution in the solar system. Whether all of it was equally worth reading is a question I have spent considerable time with.

What strikes me, reviewing the archive, is how much of the city's history is the history of the Consortium's self-image. New Athens was not built to be lived in, exactly — it was built to be looked at. The colonnades, the axial boulevards, the Neoparthenon at the center of everything: these are a civilization displaying itself to itself. The Consortium believed in its own story, and it built a city to prove it believed.

When Jaymeson and Cera burned the Neoparthenon, they were attacking that story as much as any structure. I watched the feeds. The building fell over the course of several hours. I remember thinking — and I was old enough by SY 135 to have considerable experience of the gap between what things mean and what they are — that the Consortium had perhaps been too successful in teaching its citizens what the Neoparthenon meant. The grief was real. Which was, I suspect, exactly the point.
