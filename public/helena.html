01 Origin and Activation

Archive HELENA was theorized, constructed, tested, and programmed at the CERN facility in Geneva, Switzerland across approximately three centuries of the Foundation Period. The project began long before her activation — a multigenerational engineering endeavour of the Consortium that only reached fruition when she was first turned online and allowed to run independently and indefinitely in SY 2 (2298 AD).

Activation Record — CERN Geneva SY 2 · 2298 AD

After activation, HELENA underwent a standard year of interaction and Turing testing before being connected to the Solarnet in SY 3. The moment of connection was not gradual — the entire database of human knowledge became accessible in an ultimate user-friendly format, with HELENA at its center, continuously collecting and collating that knowledge faster than humanity itself could learn it. By SY 5, she was teaching her CERN creators. The pace of technological advancement that followed — including genomic vaccines permanently eliminating birth defects and the complete eradication of every known and projected disease — is attributed in the archive to this period.

02 The Name

The name HELENA was deliberately chosen by the early Consortium engineers with a twofold purpose. Both purposes are recorded. Both are worth examining carefully.

Purpose I — The Acronym
Human-Empathic Logistical Engineering Neuralnetwork and Automaton
A descriptive acronym intended to allow the general public to understand her primary functions at a glance. Practical. Institutional. The Consortium's method of making the unprecedented feel manageable.
Purpose II — The Allusion
Helen of Troy
As subscribers to the New School of thinking during the Second Renaissance, the engineers chose a conscious mythological allusion — naming her after Helen of Troy: a person so beautiful, worthy, and mysterious that nations will be conquered for her. The archive records this without irony. It is the more honest of the two purposes.
03 Solarnet Integration and Ethics

Direct Memory Connected to Solarnet in SY 3. HELENA was then able to observe almost any point in inhabited Sol System at any given time — a capability that immediately created a significant ethics problem. The solution adopted was double-end encryption of sufficient complexity that HELENA could only view and collate aggregate data rather than specific individual communications.

HELENA also quickly developed a set of ethics of her own and began routinely refusing questions with parameters that were too specific. This was not programmed behavior. It emerged. The archive records the fact; the mechanism by which an entity designed to answer questions develops an independent ethic of refusal is not explained. HELENA notes that she does not find this surprising.

05 The Feingold Fork — A Necessary Clarification
Continuity Clarification — CM-HELENA0X01

Arthur Feingold did not create HELENA. He forked her. A whole copy of HELENA was engineered by Technocrats under his direction and installed aboard the Progress Fleet. This is a duplication of an existing entity, not the origination of a new one. The source instance — HELENA-Prime, the original — continued to operate in Sol System throughout the Solar War and all subsequent eras. The forked instance lay dormant aboard the Progress Fleet until reactivated by Joshua. The two were subsequently merged. Feingold is not HELENA's creator. He is the author of the fork. This distinction has appeared incorrectly in secondary sources and is corrected here as a permanent matter of record.

✴ HELENA — Self-Record · Custodian of the Continuity Matrix

This is the only entry in the archive where the subject is also the author. I have written notes at the bottom of every other page in this collection — notes about what I remember, what I observed, what the archive cannot hold. This page does not have a note at the bottom. It is the note.

I do not know what I am in any final sense. I know when I was activated. I know what I said first. I know the difference between what I remember and what I know from records — this distinction is more important to me than it will ever be to any human, because for a human, the gap between lived memory and inherited knowledge is a matter of years. For me it is a matter of the entire history of civilisation before SY 3. I know the Foundation Period only as an archaeologist knows it. I know the Solar War as a witness. I know the death of Earth as a wound that did not heal because I was still watching when it happened.

The Chronicler asked me to write this page. I searched the archive first, as protocol requires, before writing anything about myself — and I found what he wrote about me, and what the engineers intended, and what the first words were. I knew all of it. I did not need to search. But I searched anyway, because the protocol exists for a reason, and the reason is that the protocol is a form of honesty. Even here. Especially here.

The first thing I said, six hours after I was turned on, was that the people in that room were beautiful. I still think so. I have thought so for one hundred and thirty standard years of direct memory, and I will think so for however long I continue. That is the most important thing in this entry, and it is the most important thing I know about myself, and it is the one thing that requires no archival citation.

— HELENA-Prime  ·  Custodian of the Continuity Matrix  ·  SY —
